## What Is ManyChat?

ManyChat is a **chat automation platform** (official Meta partner) that automates Instagram DMs, comments, story replies, WhatsApp, and Messenger. It lets you respond to comments, DMs, and story replies automatically — 24/7 without manual effort.

**Why it matters for Sarah's art business:**
- Turns Instagram engagement into real leads and sales
- Captures emails directly inside DMs (no link-in-bio friction)
- Works for small accounts — even 3 extra comments can double your engagement rate
- Officially allowed by Instagram/Meta (not a grey-area tool)

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. Contacts
Everyone who interacts with your automation becomes a Contact in ManyChat. You can tag them, add custom fields (email, name, interest), and segment them for future broadcasts.

### 2. Triggers
The event that starts an automation. Main Instagram triggers:
- **User comments on a post/reel** (most powerful for reach)
- **User sends a DM** (keyword-based or AI intent detection)
- **User replies to a story**
- **User clicks an Instagram ad** (Pro only)
- **New follower** (Pro only — auto-DM new followers)

### 3. Automations (Flows)
Where you spend 99% of your time. A flow = trigger → messages → actions.

Each automation starts with a trigger, then chains:
- Instagram DM messages
- Quick reply buttons
- Collect user data (email, name)
- Conditions (if/else branching)
- Smart delays
- Actions (tag contact, send to email platform, open URL)
- AI steps (intent detection)

### 4. Live Chat
When automation can't handle it, conversations go to Live Chat for manual reply. Best practice: set automation pause to 24h when you manually respond so your flow doesn't interrupt.

### 5. Broadcasting
Send a message to all (or segmented) contacts. Great for art drops, sales, announcements.

## The Core Playbook (Comment → DM → Email)

This is the single most effective flow for art creators:

**Step 1: Post a reel/post with CTA**
> "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you [thing]"

Examples for Sarah:
- "Comment PRINTS and I'll send you my current print shop link"
- "Comment PROCESS and I'll send you my behind-the-scenes video"
- "Comment COMMISSIONS and I'll send you commission info + pricing"

**Step 2: Trigger fires**
User comments keyword → ManyChat sends them a DM automatically

**Step 3: First DM message**
Warm, personal. One message + one button only (Instagram restriction on first message).
> "Hey! So happy you're interested 🎨 Click below and I'll send it right over"
> [Button: "Send me the link!"]

**Step 4: Collect email (optional but powerful)**
> "Before I send it — want me to add you to my list so you hear about new prints first?"
> [Yes please] [No thanks]

If yes → ask for email → ask for name → send to email platform (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, etc.)

**Step 5: Send the thing**
Link, PDF, video, shop URL — whatever you promised.

## Keyword Best Practices

- Use **simple, obvious keywords** that match what you promised ("PRINTS", "GUIDE", "LINK")
- Add **variants** for typos/caps: "prints", "PRINTS", "print" — ManyChat lets you add multiple
- Use **"message begins with"** for DMs (most reliable)
- Use **"message contains"** only for rare/obscure keywords
- Keywords are **case-sensitive by default** — add upper and lower variants
- Pro tip: Use the **AI intent detection** trigger for story replies where wording varies

---

## Flow Design Best Practices

**Keep it short.** 1-3 steps max for most automations. Long flows = people drop off.

**Be transparent.** Tell people it's automated. "Comment X and I'll automatically send you Y." Trust > trickery.

**One ask at a time.** Don't ask for email + name + phone in one message. One step, then the next.

**Match the vibe.** Sarah's flows should feel warm and artistic, not corporate. Use her voice.

**Smart delays.** Add a 30-60 second delay before your DM so it doesn't feel instant-robotic.

## Advanced Features (When Sarah's Ready)

### AI Intent Detection
Instead of exact keywords, ManyChat uses AI to understand what someone means. Good for story replies where wording varies wildly.

### Conditional Logic
"If they said YES to email, do X. If NO, do Y." Branch flows based on responses.

### Sequences
Multi-step drip campaigns inside DMs. Example: 3-day "welcome to my world" sequence for new followers.

### DM New Followers (Pro)
Auto-DM anyone who follows you. High touch, immediate connection. Must be warm/non-spammy.

### Split Testing
Test two different opening messages and see which gets better reply rates.

### CRM Integration
Push collected leads directly to Google Sheets, HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, etc.

### Story-Specific Flows (STD Strategy — Brock Johnson)
Post a story with heavy text ending in "Reply [KEYWORD] and I'll send you X." DM replies to stories are the **highest-quality engagement signal** for Instagram's algorithm. This directly boosts story views.

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't automate 100% of everything — feels spammy, kills trust
- ❌ Don't build 17-step flows for simple giveaways — people bounce
- ❌ Don't use vague keywords that trigger on accident ("hi", "yes", "love")
- ❌ Don't ignore Live Chat — some conversations need a human
- ❌ Don't forget to test before going live (always test with a second account)
