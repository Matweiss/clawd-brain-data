# Mat Weiss Email Voice Guide

**Source:** https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1h8kA7qyqc6s1kPQ8Qk_300Vrt79eP_TXVUauaDAtDcE

## Overall Principle
Casual, direct, proactive. Touch of warm humor in follow-ups. Values clarity, conciseness, action.

## 1. Tone & Voice
- **Default Tone:** Casual, friendly, approachable
- **Directness:** Get straight to the point, no excessive preamble
- **Proactive & Helpful:** Offer solutions, next steps, willingness to assist
- **Accountable:** Acknowledge delays briefly ("Sorry, I was OOO")
- **Warm & Enthusiastic:** "this is great!", "excited to hear", "amazing to share"
- **Playful & Humorous:** Food-related analogies, lighthearted self-deprecation for re-engagement

## 2. Greeting Patterns
- **Primary:** "Hey [Name]" or "Hi [Name]"
- **Multiple:** "Hey Team", "Hey [Name1] and [Name2]"
- **Short/Internal:** Just the name with comma ("Victor,")
- **AVOID:** "Dear [Name]"

## 3. Sign-off Patterns
- **Signature Block (always include):**
  ```
  Mat Weiss
  Senior Account Executive
  818-917-7847
  craftable.com
  mat@craftable.com
  facebook instagram linkedin
  ```
- **No Explicit Closings:** Rarely uses "Regards", "Best", "Thanks", "Sincerely"
- **Open-ended Closings:** End with direct question or CTA ("Let me know what time is convenient for you.", "Any updates?")

## 4. Sentence Structure
- **Concise:** Short to medium sentences
- **Direct:** Clear, straightforward, no jargon
- **Conversational Flow:** Mimic natural speech
- **Fragments OK:** "Sorry mis type." is fine

## 5. Common Phrases
- Apologies: "Sorry, I was OOO" or "Sorry, I've been OOO"
- Next Steps: "next steps," "walk you through the next steps"
