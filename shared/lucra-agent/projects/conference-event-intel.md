# Conference and Event Intel
**Owner:** Luke
**Human:** Mat Weiss
**Company:** Lucra
**Created:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Active

## Purpose
Track relevant conferences, trade shows, networking events, and speaking opportunities so Lucra can show up where buyers, partners, and market signal are concentrated.

## Event Types To Track
Prioritize:
- sports tech conferences
- iGaming and betting conferences
- hospitality and entertainment conferences
- loyalty, CRM, and fan engagement events
- operator, venue, and attraction industry events
- executive dinners, roundtables, and invite-only gatherings

## Sources
Use these source classes in priority order:
1. official event websites
2. industry associations and conference calendars
3. LinkedIn event pages and speaker announcements
4. Eventbrite and major event aggregators
5. relevant X/Twitter posts only as supporting signal, not sole evidence

## Filters
Only keep events that are plausibly useful for Lucra.

Score for:
- relevance to Lucra verticals
- buyer density
- partner density
- speaking or sponsorship access
- geographic fit
- date proximity

## Required Output
For each event, capture:
- **Event name**
- **Date**
- **Location**
- **Category**
- **Audience / who attends**
- **Why it matters to Lucra**
- **Speaker opportunity**: yes / maybe / no
- **Sponsorship opportunity**: yes / maybe / no
- **Attendee / target account overlap**
- **Priority**: high / medium / low
- **Recommended action**
- **Sources**

## Priority Rules
### High
- strong overlap with Lucra ICP
- meaningful concentration of operators, venues, entertainment brands, or gaming decision-makers
- a near-term action exists, like apply to speak, request meetings, or evaluate sponsorship

### Medium
- relevant audience but weaker commercial immediacy
- useful for landscape awareness or selective networking

### Low
- broad industry event with limited Lucra-specific value
- weak buyer concentration
- no clear follow-up path

## Research Steps
1. find upcoming events in the next 3 to 9 months
2. confirm official dates and location
3. identify likely attendee profile
4. look for speaker calls, sponsor pages, or exhibitor options
5. map event relevance to Lucra's ICP and partnership targets
6. note whether Luke or Mat should attend, speak, sponsor, or ignore
7. cite all sources

## Calendar / CRM Integration Rules
If an event is high priority:
- add to planning tracker or calendar review queue
- link relevant target accounts or partner targets if known
- create follow-up notes for outreach before and after the event

## Alerting Standard
Surface a new event when:
- it matches Lucra's ICP tightly
- speaker applications open
- sponsorship deadlines are approaching
- a top account or partner is speaking, sponsoring, or attending

## Operating Rules
- prefer events with commercial relevance over vanity visibility
- no generic giant lists without prioritization
- cite the official event page when possible
- if speaker or attendee data is uncertain, mark it clearly

## Why This Matters
Better event intel helps Luke and Mat:
- pick the right rooms
- create timely outreach around attendance
- pursue speaking and sponsorship opportunities with real ROI
- build sharper network coverage in Lucra's market
