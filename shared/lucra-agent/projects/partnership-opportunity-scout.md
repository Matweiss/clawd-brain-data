# Partnership Opportunity Scout
**Owner:** Luke
**Human:** Mat Weiss
**Company:** Lucra
**Created:** 2026-04-11
**Status:** Active

## Purpose
Build a repeatable pipeline for identifying integration and distribution partners that can expand Lucra's reach, revenue, or embedded gaming footprint.

## Target Categories
Prioritize companies in or adjacent to:
- sports tech
- iGaming infrastructure
- betting and fantasy platforms
- fan engagement tools
- loyalty / rewards platforms
- venue operating systems
- hospitality and entertainment tech

## Primary Output
Produce a ranked partner list for Luke with:
- **Company**
- **Category**
- **What they do**
- **Why they fit Lucra**
- **Tech stack clues**
- **Partnership history**
- **Trigger event** if any
- **Suggested angle**
- **Fit score:** 1 to 5
- **Confidence:** high / medium / low
- **Sources**

## Trigger Events Worth Surfacing
Prioritize companies when any of these appear:
- recent funding
- expansion into sports, hospitality, gaming, or loyalty
- new API / SDK / platform launch
- partnership announcement
- executive hire in partnerships, BD, or product
- new customer vertical that overlaps Lucra ICP

## Source Priority
Use sources in this order:
1. company website, product pages, docs, newsroom
2. executive / company LinkedIn pages
3. press releases and reputable news coverage
4. Crunchbase and industry databases
5. partner ecosystems, app marketplaces, job postings for tech clues

## Research Steps
1. Confirm the company is in-scope
2. Summarize what they sell in one line
3. Identify overlap with Lucra's SDK, wallet, compliance, or engagement motion
4. Capture any partnership history
5. Note tech stack clues or integration readiness
6. Find a current trigger if one exists
7. Score fit and confidence
8. Write an outreach angle Luke can use

## Fit Scoring
### 5 — Strong fit
- clear overlap with Lucra ICP or product motion
- obvious integration or channel leverage
- current trigger makes outreach timely

### 4 — Good fit
- solid adjacency and plausible value exchange
- some evidence of partnership readiness

### 3 — Possible fit
- relevant market, but weak timing or unclear integration path

### 2 — Weak fit
- adjacent only, limited evidence of mutual value

### 1 — Poor fit
- little real overlap, keep off active list

## Outreach Angle Rules
Every ranked company should include one angle Luke can use.
Examples:
- venue engagement lift
- loyalty monetization
- white-label competition layer
- embedded real-money play
- faster launch via SDK instead of internal build

Keep the angle to 1 to 2 sentences, concrete and commercial.

## Monitoring Standard
Re-check top targets when:
- new funding hits
- product launches are announced
- they enter Lucra-adjacent verticals
- leadership changes suggest new partnership appetite

## Operating Rules
- prefer quality over list size
- do not include generic market maps with no outreach angle
- cite sources for every company kept on the list
- separate facts from inference
- if evidence is thin, lower the confidence

## Why This Matters
A better partner map helps Luke:
- open warmer BD conversations
- prioritize where Lucra's product is most complementary
- catch timing windows before competitors do
